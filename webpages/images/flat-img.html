Looking at the increased sales after reduced circle rates, this has been getting extended for every six months; let’s see what will be the next move.     

The properties in Delhi have been divided into categories from A to H where the “A” category counts in the posh areas in Delhi whereas the “H” category includes the least developed areas. After the scheme, the areas covered in the A category have seen a fall in price from Rs 7.74 lakh per square meter to Rs 6.19 lakh per square meter and the H category has seen a fall in price from Rs 23,280 to Rs 18,624 per square meter.

Well, do you know what exactly the Circle rate in Delhi is?

Circle rate is the minimum price that you have to pay when buying land, flats, or apartments in the area. It varies from one area to another.

Knowing some of the factors impacting circle rate in Delhi

  • Delhi circle rates are higher when more facilities are available.
  • In Delhi, the circle rate is higher the higher the market value.
  • Posh area circle rates are always higher than others.
  • Commercial properties circle rates are higher than residential.
